A

Accord for the protection of Species at Risk
an agreement among all provinces, territories and three federal departments to commit to a national approach for species at risk.
Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/species-risk-public-registry/glossary-terms.html
Action plan
an action plan is a document that defines the projects or activities required to meet the goals and objectives outlined in the recovery strategy for a wildlife species.
Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/species-risk-public-registry/glossary-terms.html
Adipose fin
The fleshy appendage found on salmon and trout, located between the dorsal fin and the tail.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/rec/definitions-eng.html
Administrative agreement
allows for the administration of any provision in the Act, in which the competent minister has responsibility.
Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/species-risk-public-registry/glossary-terms.html

Allele(s)
Alternate forms of a gene or region of DNA. An example of alleles of a gene would be the gene for human blood types the different alleles (versions) of this gene give rise to the A, B and O blood types (AB blood type occurs when an individual has one A allele and one B allele).
Source: https://www.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/science/biotech-genom/more-plus/glossary-glossaire-eng.html
Alternative measures agreement
allows for the provision of alternative measures to deal with persons alleged to have committed offences, but only if it is consistent with the purposes of the Act.
Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/species-risk-public-registry/glossary-terms.html
Amplified Fragment Length Polymorphisms
A genome survey technique to detect polymorphic regions in DNA (regions of the DNA that have at least two alleles).
Source: https://www.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/science/biotech-genom/more-plus/glossary-glossaire-eng.html
Annual limit
The total amount of fish of a certain species that may be caught and retained during the course of a fishing year which commences on April 1 and ends the following March 31.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/rec/definitions-eng.html
Aquaculture
The farming of aquatic organisms in the marine environment or freshwater. For further information see the DFO Aquaculture site.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
Aquatic invertebrates
An invertebrate is an animal that lacks a backbone. An aquatic invertebrate is an invertebrate that lives in water. Examples of aquatic invertebrates include mussels, clams, coral, sponges and marine worms.
Source: https://www.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/science/biotech-genom/more-plus/glossary-glossaire-eng.html
Aquatic species
a wildlife species that is a fish as defined in section 2 of the Fisheries Act, or a marine plant as defined in section 47 of the Act.
Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/species-risk-public-registry/glossary-terms.html
Area
A statistical area as defined by the Pacific Fishery Management Area Regulations 2007.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/rec/definitions-eng.html

Axillary appendage
A small fleshy protuberance at the base of the pelvic fin of salmon and trout. It is used to distinguish salmonids from other fishes with adipose fins like the smelts such as eulachon.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html

B

Bar rig
A line or leader with a weight no greater than 1 kg attached at the end, and from which no more than two lines are attached by means of one or more swivels, spreader bars or other means. Each of these two lines may have only one hook attached.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/rec/definitions-eng.html
Barbless hook
A hook from which all barbs have been removed—either filed off or pinched flat against the shaft. The shaft of a hook is the straight part between the eye and the bend.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/rec/definitions-eng.html
Biodiversity or biological diversity
The full range of variety and variability within and among living organisms and the ecological complexes in which they occur; and encompasses diversity at the ecosystem, community, species, and genetic levels and the interaction of these components.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
Biomass
Biomass, or the index of biomass, is the total weight of a given species of fish in one area at a specific time. This can be for a full population or a defined portion of it. The time periods are usually the time of the survey or measurement. Biomass maximum sustainable yield is the biomass level for a species where harvest of this species is maximized. This is influenced by the health of the fish population and other environmental factors.
Source: https://www.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/reports-rapports/regs/sff-cpd/survey-sondage/gloss-en.html

Border escapement
The number of adult, upstream migrating salmon that escape all U.S. fisheries and reach the Canada/U.S. border.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
Breeding program
Refers to the planned breeding of organisms (animals or plants) over several generations and does not include direct changes to the organism's genes (or DNA). This type of breeding is usually done to get offspring with more desirable traits.
Source: https://www.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/science/biotech-genom/more-plus/glossary-glossaire-eng.html
Brood year
The parental year for a group of returning salmon, i.e. the calendar year when the majority of parents of these fish spawned.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
Broodstock
Mature salmon from which milt and roe are extracted to produce the next generation of cultivated fish.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
By-catch
Incidental or unintentional catch of non-target stocks or species.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html

Catch and release
A conservation measure that requires that if a fish is caught, it must be returned to the water. Similar terms include “closed to retention,” “non-retention” and “daily limit is 0.”
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/rec/definitions-eng.html
Catch Per Unit Effort
Catch per unit effort. Need definition.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
Cautious zone
A stock is considered to be in the cautious zone if the biomass, or the index of biomass, is higher than the limit reference point but lower than the upper stock reference point. This means it’s greater than 40% of biomass maximum sustainable yield but lower than 80% of the yield.
Source: https://www.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/reports-rapports/regs/sff-cpd/survey-sondage/gloss-en.html
Circle hook
A hook in which the shank, bend and point form an almost enclosed circle, with the tip pointing towards the shaft at roughly 90 degrees. Circle hooks may not be offset more than 10 degrees. The shape of the circle hook makes it difficult for the hook to set except in the fish’s jaw. Should a hook be ingested, it may be drawn back out of the fish’s gut without engaging.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/rec/definitions-eng.html
Coded wire tag
Microscopic stainless steel wire tag that is inserted in the snout of juvenile Chinook and Coho salmon (usually hatchery stock) prior to release or before ocean migration; the tag has encoded information that indicates the origin and year of release of the fish. Tagging and recovery using CWTs is an important tool for stock assessment and fishery management decisions to protect stocks at risk and guide sustainable salmon fisheries along the west coast of Canada and the USA.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html

Communal commercial licence
Issued to First Nations' organizations pursuant to the Aboriginal Communal Fishing Licences Regulations for participation in the general commercial fishery. Licences issued are equivalent to the capacity of licences that have been retired under the Aboriginal Fisheries Strategy (AFS) Licence Retirement/Allocation Transfer Program.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
Communal licence
Issued to First Nations' organizations pursuant to the Aboriginal Communal Fishing Licences Regulations to carry on fishing and related activities. For further information see the Fisheries and Oceans Canada web-site.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
Competent Minister
a. the Minister responsible for the Parks Canada Agency with respect to individuals in or on federal lands administered by that Agency; b. the Minister of Fisheries and Oceans with respect to aquatic species, other than individuals mentioned in paragraph (a); and c. the Minister of the Environment with respect to all other individuals.
Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/species-risk-public-registry/glossary-terms.html
Conservation
The protection, maintenance, and rehabilitation of genetic diversity, species, and ecosystems to sustain biodiversity and the continuance of evolutionary and natural production processes.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
Conservation harvesting plan
Conservation harvesting plans are fishing plans that identify harvesting methods aimed at minimizing the harvest of small fish and bycatch of groundfish. Plans typically include measures to control and monitor: gear restrictions, time and area closures, both target and bycatch species, bycatch and discarding protocols, monitoring and logbook requirements.
Source: https://www.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/reports-rapports/regs/sff-cpd/survey-sondage/gloss-en.html
Conservation Unit
A Conservation Unit (CU) is a group of wild Pacific salmon sufficiently isolated from other groups that, if extirpated, is very unlikely to recolonize naturally within an acceptable timeframe, such as a human lifetime or a specified number of salmon generations.
Source: https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/bfeb3ecd-b99a-4d1a-b8c1-87373f31f4bd
Critical habitat
the habitat that is necessary for the survival or recovery of a listed wildlife species and that is identified as the species critical habitat in the recovery strategy or in an action plan for the species.
Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/species-risk-public-registry/glossary-terms.html
Critical zone
A stock is considered to be in the critical zone if the mature biomass, or its index, is less than the limit reference point. This means it’s less than or equal to 40% of biomass maximum sustainable yield, which is where serious harm is likely occurring to the stock.
Source: https://www.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/reports-rapports/regs/sff-cpd/survey-sondage/gloss-en.html
Cumulative Effects
The term “cumulative effects” generally refers to the combined effects from past, present, and reasonably foreseeable future activities and natural processes. Specific definitions vary among different parties and under different legislation and policies, but the term generally refers to effects that may be individually minor, but collectively significant. Effects can be adverse (e.g., decreased water quality in a regional river) or positive (e.g., socioeconomic benefits like jobs and business for a local community).
Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/cumulative-effects.html
Cut banks
Refers to situations where the stream has undercut its bank through erosion. The habitat that is created is often heavily used by large salmonids like cutthroat and rainbow trout.
Source: https://www.pac.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/fm-gp/salmon-saumon/gloss-eng.html
